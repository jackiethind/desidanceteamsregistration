Meet the Instructors!

Master Class

Brittnie Aguilar

Brittnie Aguilar was born and raised in Hawaii and started dancing at Hypersquad Dance Co at the age of 7. She was a part of Academy of Hype, achieving 1st place at World of Dance Hawaii 2010 & World of Dance LA 2011. Additionally, Brittnie was also involved in Hype 5-0 which was awarded 3rd place on America's Best Dance Crew Season 5. In Hawaii, Brittnie founded Blank Canvas Crew, opening The Playground Dance Studio. At the end of 2014, Brittnie moved to California to pursue her dream team, GRV. GRV has achieved 2nd place at Vibe 2015, 3rd place at Bridge 2016, and cool points at Body Rock. Brittnie is currently a choreographer for GRV& continues her dance career through traveling and teaching.

Gopi Engineer

Gopi Engineer is a dance artist, choreographer, instructor and performer whose dance journey spans over 30​ years in Bollywood, Indian folk and Indian classical dance along with contemporary and fusion styles. In the folk genre, she has engaged in garba, raas, lavani and bhangra dance styles and in the classical dance she has trained in both Kathak and Bharatanatyam. As the founder and Artistic director of Meher Dance Company, Gopi teaches Bollywood and Indian dance to kids and adults in the Chicago land area, leads a professional dance troupe, teaches private choreography in person and online as well as engages in dance production​ projects internationally. She was the dance coordinator for the Bollywood film, Dhoom 3 and was the director of choreography for the Bengali film, Simanaheen. This past year, her company ​was featured in Buzzfeed Video's film "The Art of Dance" showcasing movement in 360 videography, the first of its kind. She is truly passionate about dance and advancing this art form, but nothing brings her more joy than to teach people how to dance and to encourage great talent like what is seen in DDN.

Lavesh Pritami

Lavesh began teaching Bhangra at Natya Academy in North Carolina in 2006. Over the course of 10 years, he taught over 300 students and also started the independent (non-collegiate) competitive Bhangra movement in NC. He established over 20 teams (youth and competitive) and performed across North America at cultural, diversity, corporate, competitive, and social events. In 2006, Lavesh won his first competition in NYC with his team Gabroo Alliance, a mixture of music and live (Dhol). He also was a part of UNC Bhangra Elite during the years of 2006 - 2010 where he competed twice and placed 2nd at a competition. In 2007, Lavesh started Gajjde Gabroo, an all-male live (Dhol) team that competed until 2010. The team competed in Virginia, California, Toronto, and also won first place in Texas. In 2008, Lavesh created a "junior" co-ed music team called Gajjdi Jawani, which also competed across the USA. Lavesh coached Gajjdi Jawani to win a local competition in NC and also competed numerous times in NYC, Virginia, California, and NC. The team recently rebranded to North Carolina Di Tohr and continues to compete under his guidance. In Nov. of 2014, Lavesh and his team launched the Learn Bhangra App for iOS and Android devices. In just two years, the App achieved over 40K downloads and 15K registered users! The App continues to grow at a rate of ~1.5K downloads a month and commands a strong Social Media presence with a 100K+ weekly user reach. Additionally, Lavesh conducts Bhangra workshops internationally on a frequent basis. He has conducted workshops in Mexico (three times), India, China, NYC, North Carolina, Atlanta (twice), Argentina (multiple locations), Colombia, Singapore, Los Angeles, and Chicago. For 2017, he has workshops booked in Virginia, Berlin, Mexico City, Brazil, Istanbul, Argentina (multiple locations), Spain, Seoul, London, Kuala Lumpur, Australia (multiple locations), and many more to be announced! In 2015-16, Lavesh worked with the Jus Punjabi network to create a “Learn Bhangra with Lavesh” TV show that broadcasted for many months. Also in 2016, Lavesh was invited to perform and teach Bhangra at the 2016 Rio Olympics along with members from the India cultural delegation. Due to his vast competitive and teaching experience, Lavesh also judges Bhangra competitions around the world. His prior experience includes judging at Aaj Ka Dhamaka (3 times – North Carolina), Nachte Raho (2 times - Iowa), ATL Tamasha (Atlanta), Nach Ke Dikha (Virginia), Temple Tashan (Philadelphia), Got Bhangra (Virginia), Bhangra Dance Off (Malaysia), East Coast Classic (Virginia), UGA India Night (Athens), Bhangra Fusion Dance Contest (Mexico), Circle City Bhangra (Indianapolis) as well as being invited to judge Bhangra Blowout (Washington DC), Bruin Bhangra (California), amongst others. Lavesh's Bhangra background derives from the PAU, College of Agriculture school of thought, under the guidance of Ustaad Janak Raj Ji. Lavesh's coach, Master Gurinderbir "Pearl" Chahal, helped him understand folk elements and transformed Lavesh's approach towards Bhangra and how the teachings of discipline, confidence, and respect incorporate into everyday life.

Varun Gurunath

Varun Gurunath is a veteran in the Bollywood dance industry. Having performed and taught for over 10 years, he is an acclaimed choreographer whose style blends Bollywood with the aesthetics of modern Hip Hop and the energy and masala of the South Indian film industry. He is currently one of the main instructors and choreographers at NDM Bollywood Dance Studios, the first Bollywood dance school in the United States. Varun's industry work includes assistant choreographer for Nakul Dev Mahajan on So You Think You Can Dance, Nickelodeon, NBC's The Office, America's Best Dance Crew, Prabhu Deva, and Natalie Portman. He strives to inspire and instill his dance expertise and knowledge to kids all over so they can love and pursue their passion.

Yuri Doolan

Yuri Doolan is a Bollywood Fusion dancer and choreographer based in Chicago, Illinois. He is most known for his work with Northwestern University’s own Bollywood Fusion team, Anubhav, and his pioneering incorporation of critical Asian American storytelling through the medium of Bollywood dance shows. Yuri’s choreography and show design has led to national titles in 2014, 2016, and 2017. Originally from Ohio—Yuri is also a former dance captain of Ohio State University’s all-male fusion dance team, Genesis. Yuri’s primary background and training is in gymnastics, rather dance. As an outsider to both dance and South Asian culture in particular, Yuri has combined the athleticism and artistry of gymnastics with the South Asian styles learnt from peers and co-leaders, to create a truly “Fusion” style that is unique to him as a choreographer. In the past, Yuri has also choreographed for high school and middle school show choirs and has danced in major productions at award-winning entertainment theme parks. Beyond dance, Yuri is a doctoral student in History and Asian American Studies at Northwestern University. He is interested in Asian American identity, community formation, cultural productions, and social movements.

Anisha Babbar

Anisha Babbar is a Bollywood Fusion dancer and choreographer. Being born and bred in a Bollywood fusion dance environment, Anisha's training is eclectic in nature. Her choreography is influenced by elements of jazz, hip hop, bharatanatyam, Indian folk, and modern Bollywood. Anisha has dubbed her characteristic fusion choreography style as Bollywood Funk. Anisha got an early start as a Bollywood dancer at the age of 12 by becoming a professional dancer for Naach Dance Company in 2006. At 15, she went on to become a professional dancer and youngest instructor for Mona Khan Company--one of the biggest Bollywood Dance companies in North America. From there, her journey as a choreographer took off with her tenure as the lead dancer and head choreographer of UC Berkeley Azaad for the 2014 and 2015 seasons. She led the team to many first place titles and also acquired several awards for Best Choreography and Best Female Dancer from prestigious competitions such as Jhoomti Shaam, Tufaan, and Bollywood America. Anisha's performance repertoire includes events like the Super Bowl 50 halftime show, NBC's America's Got Talent, NBA halftime shows, and performing with Bollywood celebrities such as Shahrukh Khan and Shaan. Today she works as a choreographer and dancer for her YouTube channel, which has produced several high production choreography videos, one of which was recognized and shared by Yash Raj Films. Anisha has many artistic inspirations including Michael Jackson, Madhuri Dixit, Remo D'souza, and Mia Michaels. She owes much of her training to her acclaimed instructors over the years: Mona Sampath Khan, Ishika Seth, Shannon Hanly, and Brian Brooks, to name a few. Their tutelage has largely shaped Anisha's creative voice and artistic identity. As for her professional life, Anisha graduated from UC Berkeley in 2015 with a double major in Public Health and Economics. Today she works in healthcare data analytics and is concurrently pursuing a Master’s degree in Health Law and Policy from UCSF. She dances part-time and hopes to grow her dance endeavors alongside her career in healthcare.

Jeril Lukose

Jeril Lukose is one of the choreographers for The MOD Project based in the city of Chicago. His primary responsibility for his crew is to choreograph the Dappanguthu (Kuthu for short) elements in their routine, and has been doing so for the last 4 years. He started competing in the Desi Dance Circuit with Chicago Dhamaal in the 2012-2013 dance season before becoming one of the founding fathers of The Men of Dance Project in late 2013. However, he had his beginnings performing on stage at various ISA shows and cultural shows in high school and undergrad. The Kuthu style of dance that he has grown to love since childhood and brings to life on stage comes from the inspiration of dancers from the South Indian movie industries such as Prabhu Deva, Raghava Lawrence, and Jr. NTR. He hopes to bring a greater understanding and love towards the Kuthu dance style found in the Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and Kannada movie industries at DDCon 2017!
